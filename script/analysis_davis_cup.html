Count data model

  • The year is included centered and as a quadratic polynomial
  • Two slopes are included for middle age (before and after 27 years)

Forest plot

Next figure shows the incidence Rate Ratio for the different factors in study.

Regarding the surfaces, the rate of retirements in Clay, Hard or Grass is more than double than in the carpet surface, being the two formers statistically significant.

The behavior of retirements is different depending on the age of participants. If they are younger (mean age below 27), then the incidence rate (IR) of retirements is the half for a 5 years increase. Meanwhile, if the mean age of the players is at least 27 years, then the IR doubles for 5 years increase, but not statistical significantly.

Finally, the number of individual match also plays a role in the retirements but it depends on the number of sets for the eliminatory: - If the matches are at best of 3 sets, retirements are less usual in 3rd and 4th matches. - Oppositely, if the matches are at best of 5 sets, retirements are more prone to be produced in 3rd and 4th matches.
